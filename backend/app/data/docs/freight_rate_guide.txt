LOGISTICS AI TRACKER — FREIGHT RATE GUIDE
==========================================

1. INTRODUCTION
----------------
Understanding freight rates is essential for effective supply-chain cost management.
This guide explains how rates are structured on the Logistics AI Tracker platform,
the surcharges that may apply, seasonal pricing patterns, and practical negotiation
strategies to help shippers secure the best possible rates.

2. RATE STRUCTURE OVERVIEW
---------------------------
Freight rates on the platform are quoted as a per-kilogram charge in US Dollars (USD).
The base rate covers the primary transport leg from origin to destination. Rates vary
by several factors:

  • Transport Mode:
    - Sea Freight: Typically the most cost-effective for heavy or bulk cargo.
      Rates range from $0.70/kg to $2.50/kg depending on route and carrier.
    - Air Freight: Premium service for time-sensitive or high-value cargo.
      Rates range from $3.50/kg to $8.00/kg. Express services can exceed $10/kg.
    - Road Freight: Competitive for intra-continental routes (e.g., Europe, North
      America). Rates range from $0.50/kg to $1.50/kg.
    - Rail Freight: An emerging alternative for China–Europe corridors. Faster than
      sea, cheaper than air. Rates range from $1.00/kg to $2.00/kg.

  • Route / Trade Lane:
    High-volume trade lanes (e.g., Shanghai–Los Angeles, Rotterdam–Singapore) tend
    to have lower rates due to carrier competition and economies of scale. Niche or
    low-volume routes carry a premium.

  • Cargo Weight & Volume:
    Rates improve with higher volumes. The platform supports volume-based discounts:
      - Under 100 kg: Standard rate.
      - 100–1,000 kg: 5% discount on base rate.
      - 1,000–10,000 kg: 10% discount on base rate.
      - Over 10,000 kg: Custom quote — contact sales for negotiated pricing.

  • Carrier Selection:
    Premium carriers with better reliability metrics (on-time performance, damage
    rates) typically charge 10–20% above market average. Budget carriers offer
    savings but may have longer transit times or fewer schedule options.

3. SURCHARGES & ACCESSORIAL FEES
----------------------------------
The base rate does not include the following surcharges, which are applied as
applicable:

  • Bunker Adjustment Factor (BAF): Covers fuel cost fluctuations for sea freight.
    Currently ranges from $50–$200 per TEU depending on oil prices.

  • Currency Adjustment Factor (CAF): Applied when the billing currency differs from
    the carrier's base currency. Typically 2–5% of the base rate.

  • Peak Season Surcharge (PSS): Applied during high-demand periods (see Section 4).
    Can add $200–$500 per TEU for sea freight; 15–30% on air freight rates.

  • Security Surcharge: Post-9/11 mandatory charge for air cargo. Flat $0.05/kg.

  • Dangerous Goods Surcharge: For IMDG/IATA-classified hazardous materials. Varies
    by class; typically $150–$500 per shipment.

  • Overweight / Over-dimensional Surcharge: For cargo exceeding standard container
    weight limits or requiring special equipment (flat-racks, open-tops).

  • Terminal Handling Charges (THC): Port-side handling at origin and destination.
    Ranges from $100–$300 per TEU.

  • Customs Brokerage Fees: For customs clearance facilitation. Flat fee of $50–$150
    per shipment or ad valorem (0.5% of cargo value).

  • Inland Haulage: Door-to-port or port-to-door trucking charges. Quoted separately
    based on distance and local rates.

4. SEASONAL VARIATIONS
------------------------
Freight rates are subject to significant seasonal fluctuations:

  • Pre-Chinese New Year (Jan–Feb): Sea freight from Asia spikes 20–40% as
    manufacturers rush to ship before factory closures.

  • Q3 Peak Season (Jul–Sep): Retailers stock up for Black Friday, Cyber Monday,
    and the holiday season. Trans-Pacific rates peak during this window.

  • Golden Week (Oct): Chinese national holiday causes a brief dip followed by a
    surge as factories reopen and clear backlogs.

  • Year-End (Nov–Dec): Air freight demand surges for e-commerce and last-minute
    holiday deliveries. Air rates can double on popular lanes.

  • Off-Peak (Mar–May): Generally the most favorable period for rate negotiation.
    Carriers offer promotional rates to fill capacity.

Shippers are advised to book 4–6 weeks in advance during peak periods and to consider
flexible sailing dates to access lower rates.

5. NEGOTIATION TIPS
---------------------
Effective freight rate negotiation can yield savings of 10–30%. Key strategies:

  a) Consolidate Volume: Aggregate shipments across business units or time periods
     to present larger volumes to carriers. Volume commitments unlock tier discounts.

  b) Multi-Carrier Strategy: Avoid over-reliance on a single carrier. Maintain
     relationships with 2–3 carriers per trade lane to foster competition.

  c) Long-Term Contracts: Annual or multi-year contracts lock in rates and protect
     against peak-season surcharges. Ideal for shippers with predictable volumes.

  d) Flexible Routing: Consider alternative ports or transshipment hubs. A slight
     detour can unlock significantly cheaper rates (e.g., Tanjung Pelepas instead
     of Singapore for Southeast Asia exports).

  e) Off-Peak Shipping: Shift non-urgent shipments to off-peak windows (Mar–May)
     for the best rates.

  f) Leverage Data: Use the platform's rate analytics dashboard to benchmark
     current rates against historical trends and market averages. Data-driven
     negotiations are more effective.

  g) Negotiate Surcharges Separately: Base rates often get the most attention,
     but surcharges (BAF, THC, PSS) can add 20–40% to the total cost. Negotiate
     caps or fixed surcharges where possible.

  h) Consider Mode-Switching: For moderately time-sensitive cargo, rail freight
     (China–Europe in 15–18 days) offers a compelling middle ground between sea
     (35–40 days) and air (2–4 days) at roughly 40–60% of air freight cost.
