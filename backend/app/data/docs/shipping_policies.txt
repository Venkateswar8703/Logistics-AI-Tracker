LOGISTICS AI TRACKER — SHIPPING POLICIES & PROCEDURES
======================================================

1. GENERAL SHIPPING POLICY
--------------------------
All shipments processed through the Logistics AI Tracker platform are subject to the
policies outlined in this document. Our platform supports four primary modes of freight
transport: Sea (ocean freight), Air (air cargo), Road (trucking / ground transport), and
Rail (intermodal rail freight). Each mode has specific handling requirements, transit time
expectations, and documentation obligations that shippers and consignees must adhere to.

Shipments are accepted on a first-come, first-served basis. Booking confirmation is issued
once all required documentation has been submitted and validated. The platform reserves
the right to refuse any shipment that does not comply with applicable international trade
regulations, hazardous materials guidelines, or customs requirements.

2. PACKAGING & HANDLING PROCEDURES
-----------------------------------
Proper packaging is the responsibility of the shipper. All goods must be packed in
containers or packaging suitable for the chosen transport mode. Key guidelines include:

  • Sea Freight: Cargo must be palletised or containerised. Loose cargo is not accepted
    unless a flat-rack or open-top container booking has been confirmed. Maximum weight
    per TEU (Twenty-foot Equivalent Unit) is 28,000 kg. Overweight containers will be
    rejected at the port gate.

  • Air Freight: Packages must conform to IATA Dangerous Goods Regulations if applicable.
    Maximum single-piece weight is 250 kg unless prior arrangement is made with the
    carrier. All air cargo must include a shipper's declaration and air waybill.

  • Road Freight: Full Truck Load (FTL) and Less-than-Truck Load (LTL) services are
    available. Goods must be secured to prevent shifting during transit. Load height must
    not exceed 2.7 metres for standard trailers.

  • Rail Freight: Containers must comply with ISO 668 standards. Intermodal containers
    are preferred. Bulk cargo rail services are available on select corridors (e.g.,
    China–Europe via the Trans-Siberian or Southern Corridor routes).

Fragile or temperature-sensitive goods require additional handling instructions submitted
at the time of booking. Failure to declare special handling requirements releases the
carrier and the platform from liability for damage incurred during transit.

3. INSURANCE POLICY
--------------------
Cargo insurance is optional but strongly recommended. The Logistics AI Tracker platform
partners with leading marine and cargo insurance providers to offer competitive coverage.
Three tiers of insurance are available:

  • Basic Coverage: Covers total loss due to vessel sinking, aircraft crash, or major
    accident. Does not cover partial loss, theft, or weather-related damage. Premium:
    0.15% of declared cargo value.

  • Standard Coverage: Covers total loss, partial loss, theft, and water damage.
    Excludes damage due to improper packaging or inherent vice. Premium: 0.35% of
    declared cargo value.

  • Comprehensive Coverage: All-risk coverage including damage from delays, improper
    handling, temperature excursion, and natural disasters. Premium: 0.60% of declared
    cargo value.

Insurance claims must be filed within 30 days of delivery (or expected delivery date in
the case of lost shipments). Supporting documentation — including photographs, survey
reports, and the original bill of lading — must accompany all claims.

4. DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------
The following documents are required for international shipments:

  • Commercial Invoice: Itemised invoice with HS codes, unit values, and total value.
  • Packing List: Detailed breakdown of packages, weights, and dimensions.
  • Bill of Lading (Sea) / Air Waybill (Air) / CMR Consignment Note (Road): Transport
    document issued by the carrier.
  • Certificate of Origin: Required for tariff preference or trade agreement benefits.
  • Customs Declaration: Filed electronically via the platform's integrated customs
    module.
  • Phytosanitary / Health Certificates: Required for agricultural, food, and biological
    products.
  • Dangerous Goods Declaration: Mandatory for hazardous materials under IMDG, IATA DGR,
    or ADR regulations.

Incomplete documentation will result in shipment holds at origin or destination customs.
The platform provides automated document-checking at the time of booking to minimise
delays.

5. LIABILITY & LIMITATIONS
---------------------------
Carrier liability is governed by the applicable international convention for each mode:

  • Sea: Hague-Visby Rules — liability capped at 2 SDR per kg or 666.67 SDR per package.
  • Air: Montreal Convention — liability capped at 22 SDR per kg.
  • Road: CMR Convention — liability capped at 8.33 SDR per kg.
  • Rail: CIM/COTIF Rules — liability capped at 17 SDR per kg.

The Logistics AI Tracker platform acts as a digital freight forwarder and intermediary.
Platform liability is limited to booking accuracy and information correctness. Physical
custody liability remains with the carrier at all times.

For any disputes or policy clarifications, contact support@logistics-ai-tracker.com or
use the in-app chat assistant.
