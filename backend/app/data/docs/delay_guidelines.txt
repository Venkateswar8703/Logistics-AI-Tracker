LOGISTICS AI TRACKER — DELAY CLASSIFICATIONS & RESOLUTION GUIDELINES
=====================================================================

1. OVERVIEW
-----------
Delays are an inherent risk in global logistics. The Logistics AI Tracker platform
classifies, tracks, and resolves delays using a structured framework designed to
minimise impact on supply chains and keep all stakeholders informed in real time.
This document defines the delay taxonomy, root-cause categories, escalation paths,
and Service Level Agreement (SLA) commitments that govern how delays are managed.

2. DELAY CLASSIFICATION TIERS
-------------------------------
Every delay is assigned one of four severity tiers based on the expected duration
of the disruption relative to the original Estimated Time of Arrival (ETA):

  • Tier 1 — Minor Delay (1–24 hours):
    Causes: minor port congestion, short weather holds, documentation corrections.
    Impact: Negligible. Consignee is notified via automated alert. No SLA penalty.

  • Tier 2 — Moderate Delay (24–72 hours):
    Causes: equipment shortage, customs inspection, carrier schedule adjustment,
    moderate weather events (e.g., tropical storms en route).
    Impact: May affect downstream scheduling. Account manager is notified. Consignee
    receives updated ETA within 4 hours of delay classification.

  • Tier 3 — Significant Delay (72 hours – 7 days):
    Causes: port strikes, severe weather (typhoons, cyclones), vessel mechanical
    failure, regulatory holds, geopolitical disruptions (sanctions, embargoes).
    Impact: High. Alternative routing assessment is triggered automatically. Dedicated
    incident manager assigned. Consignee and shipper receive daily status reports.

  • Tier 4 — Critical Delay (>7 days):
    Causes: force majeure events (earthquakes, pandemics, wars), total port closure,
    vessel grounding or loss, prolonged customs seizure.
    Impact: Severe. Executive escalation within 2 hours. Insurance claim pre-filing
    initiated. Cargo rerouting or mode-switching evaluated immediately.

3. COMMON DELAY ROOT CAUSES
-----------------------------
The platform tracks the following root-cause categories for analytics and reporting:

  a) Weather & Natural Events — storms, fog, high winds, earthquakes, volcanic ash.
  b) Port & Terminal Congestion — berth unavailability, crane breakdowns, labour
     shortages, vessel bunching.
  c) Customs & Regulatory — document discrepancies, random inspections, missing
     certificates, trade compliance flags, sanctions screening holds.
  d) Carrier / Equipment — vessel mechanical failure, aircraft technical issues, truck
     breakdown, container damage, chassis shortage.
  e) Geopolitical — canal closures (e.g., Suez, Panama), sanctions, trade wars,
     regional conflicts affecting shipping lanes.
  f) Operational — booking errors, mislabelling, incorrect weight declarations,
     cargo misrouting by the carrier.
  g) Demand Surge — peak-season capacity constraints (e.g., pre-Chinese New Year,
     Black Friday, year-end), blank sailings by carriers.

4. RESOLUTION PROCEDURES
--------------------------
Upon classification of a delay, the following resolution workflow is initiated:

  Step 1 — Detection & Classification:
    The platform's tracking engine detects deviation from the planned schedule. The
    delay is auto-classified using historical data and carrier status feeds.

  Step 2 — Notification:
    Automated notifications are sent to all stakeholders (shipper, consignee, broker,
    customs agent) via email, SMS, and in-app push notifications.

  Step 3 — Root-Cause Analysis:
    The operations team (or AI engine for Tier 1–2 delays) identifies the root cause
    and logs it in the shipment record.

  Step 4 — Mitigation Planning:
    For Tier 2+ delays, a mitigation plan is created. Options include:
      • Waiting for the original service to resume.
      • Rerouting via an alternative port or corridor.
      • Mode-switching (e.g., sea-to-air for urgent cargo).
      • Splitting the shipment across multiple services.

  Step 5 — Execution & Monitoring:
    The chosen mitigation plan is executed. The platform monitors progress and updates
    the ETA dynamically.

  Step 6 — Post-Incident Review:
    After resolution, a post-incident report is generated and shared with the shipper.
    Lessons learned are fed into the platform's predictive delay model.

5. SLA COMMITMENTS
-------------------
The Logistics AI Tracker platform commits to the following SLA targets:

  • Delay Detection:      Within 1 hour of occurrence.
  • Stakeholder Notification: Within 2 hours of classification.
  • Updated ETA:          Within 4 hours of classification for Tier 2+.
  • Mitigation Plan:      Within 8 hours for Tier 3; within 4 hours for Tier 4.
  • Post-Incident Report: Within 48 hours of delay resolution.

SLA performance is measured monthly and published in the platform's analytics dashboard.
Carriers with chronic SLA violations (>3 Tier 3+ delays in a rolling 90-day window) are
flagged for review and may be deprioritised in rate recommendations.

6. COMPENSATION & CREDITS
---------------------------
  • Tier 1: No compensation.
  • Tier 2: Platform service fee waiver for the affected shipment upon request.
  • Tier 3: 15% discount on the next shipment booking + priority rebooking.
  • Tier 4: Full service fee refund + insurance claim facilitation + dedicated account
    recovery support.

Compensation claims must be submitted within 14 days of delivery (or last status update
for lost shipments). All claims are reviewed within 5 business days.

chat assistant with the query "delay status for [tracking number]".
